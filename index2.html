High-Protein Zesty Chicken and Quinoa Power Bowl


Ingredients:

  • 200g Chicken breast
  • 1 cup Quinoa, cooked
  • 1/2 Avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup Fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup Black beans, rinsed.
  • 1 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1/2 Lemon, juiced
  • 1 tsp Garlic powder and smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the Protein:  Place the diced chicken in a bowl and toss with olive oil, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  2. Sear the Chicken:    Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook for 6–8 minutes, turning occasionally, until the internal temperature reaches 74°C (165°F).
  3. Prepare the Base:    While the chicken cooks, fluff your pre-cooked quinoa and place it in a large serving bowl.
  4. Sauté the Greens:    In the last 2 minutes of cooking the chicken, toss the spinach into the pan just until it begins to wilt, then remove from heat.
  5. Assemble the Bowl: Layer the cooked chicken, wilted spinach, and black beans over the quinoa.
  6. Add Healthy Fats:   Top the bowl with the sliced avocado and drizzle the fresh lemon juice over the entire dish to help with iron absorption.
  7. Serve:                       Enjoy immediately while warm for the best texture and flavor.

Html Glosarry

Tag
The basic building block of HTML. Tags are keywords enclosed in angle brackets (like <h1>) that tell the browser how to format and display content. Most tags come in pairs: an opening tag and a closing tag (e.g., <a> and </a>).
Element
An element is the entire "object" including the opening tag, the content inside, and the closing tag. For example, <p>Hello World</p> is a paragraph element.
Attribute
Attributes provide additional information about an element. They are always placed inside the opening tag and usually come in name/value pairs, like src="image.jpg" for an image or href="index.html" for a link.
Semantic HTML
This refers to using tags that clearly describe their meaning to both the browser and the developer (e.g., <header>, <footer>, <article>). This is crucial for accessibility and Search Engine Optimization (SEO).
Boilerplate
This is the standard "starter" code required for every HTML document to function correctly. It includes the <!DOCTYPE html>, <html>, <head>, and <body> tags.
